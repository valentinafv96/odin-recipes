Rice Pudding

Description

Rice pudding is a dish made from rice mixed with water or milk and other ingredients such as cinnamon, vanilla, and raisins. Variants are used for either desserts or dinners. When used as a dessert, it is commonly combined with a sweetener such as sugar. Such desserts are found on many continents, especially Asia where rice is a staple. Some variants are thickened only with the rice starch; others include eggs, making them a kind of custard.

Ingredients

For cooking the rice

  • 1 cup of rice.
  • 2 cups of water.
  • ¼ of raw cane sugar.
  • 3 cinnamon sticks.

For the rice pudding

  • ½ l of milk.
  • 2 tablespoons of powdered milk.
  • 200 g of condensed milk: the condensed milk give this rice pudding a special kind of sweetness that differs from plain sugar. Itâ€™s creamy and almost caramelly, just divine.
  • 200 g of evaporated milk: this is a staple in Latin American cuisine and is often used with condensed milk.

For serving the rice

  • ½ teaspoon of ground cinnamon.
  • Cheese.

Steps

For cooking the rice

  1. Place everything in a large pot with a heavy bottom over medium-high heat.
  2. When it breaks to a boil, decrease the heat to a minimum and place a lid on the pot.
  3. Cook for 20 minutes and remove the lid. It doesn't matter if there's a bit of water left in the pot, it can stay there.

For the rice pudding

  1. Add the condensed and evaporated milk and stir.
  2. Add the powdered milk dissolved in the milk.
  3. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, for about 10 minutes or until the liquid can coat the back of a spoon or spatula with a thin layer.
  4. Remove from the heat and let cool down completely. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours.

For serving the rice

  1. Remove the cinnamon sticks. If it's too thick after cooling, adjust it by adding water or milk (not evaporated nor condensed).
  2. Serve cold, at room temperature or warm with a sprinkle of ground cinnamon and cheese.
