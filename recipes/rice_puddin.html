Rice Pudding

Description

Rice pudding is a dish made from rice mixed with water or milk and other ingredients such as cinnamon, vanilla, and raisins. Variants are used for either desserts or dinners. When used as a dessert, it is commonly combined with a sweetener such as sugar. Such desserts are found on many continents, especially Asia where rice is a staple. Some variants are thickened only with the rice starch; others include eggs, making them a kind of custard.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of rice.
  • 2 cups of water.
  • ¼ of raw cane sugar.
  • 3 cinnamon sticks.
  • ½ l of milk.
  • 2 tablespoons of powdered milk.
  • 200 g of condensed milk.
  • 200 g of evaporated milk.
  • ½ teaspoon of ground cinnamon.
  • Cheese.

Steps

  1. Place everything in a large pot with a heavy bottom over medium-high heat.
  2. When it breaks to a boil, decrease the heat to a minimum and place a lid on the pot.
  3. Cook for 20 minutes and remove the lid. It doesn't matter if there's a bit of water left in the pot, it can stay there.
  4. Add the condensed and evaporated milk and stir.
  5. Add the powdered milk dissolved in the milk.
  6. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, for about 10 minutes or until the liquid can coat the back of a spoon or spatula with a thin layer.
  7. Remove from the heat and let cool down completely. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  8. Remove the cinnamon sticks. If it's too thick after cooling, adjust it by adding water or milk (not evaporated nor condensed).
  9. Serve cold, at room temperature or warm with a sprinkle of ground cinnamon and cheese.
